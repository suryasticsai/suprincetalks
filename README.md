It’s where I capture the small wins, the tough conversations, the risks I ROAMed, and the lessons I learned while navigating the agile world as a SAFe Scrum Master. ---

## 📌 Why I Keep This Journal

- **To remember** – the details blur after a few sprints; this keeps them fresh.
- **To learn** – when I write down what went wrong (and right), I can spot my own blind spots.
- **To stay grounded** – it reminds me that every team, every PI, and every day is unique.
- **To share** – I know other Scrum Masters face the same challenges; maybe my stories help someone else feel less alone.

This is **my** space. It’s not a guidebook or a manifesto—it’s a living document of my growth.

---

## 🔒 How I Protect Confidentiality

I work with real people, real clients, and real sensitive data. That’s why **every entry is carefully scrubbed** before it sees the light of day.

- **Names are replaced** – "John" becomes "Team Member A", "Client X" becomes "the stakeholder".
- **Projects are generalised** – "Integrating with Salesforce" becomes "integrating with a third‑party CRM".
- **Numbers are rounded** – "3-day delay" becomes "multi-day delay", "8 story points" becomes "a typical sprint load".
- **Tools are anonymised** – I’ll say "the board" instead of "Jira Project XYZ".

The rule is simple: **if it could identify a person, a client, or a specific project, it doesn’t go in.** I share the *craft*, not the *confidentials*.

## ✍️ How I Write an Entry

Every journal entry follows a loose but consistent pattern:

1. **Start the day** – I open a new note with today’s date.
2. **Jot down key events** – stand‑up summaries, stakeholder calls, impediments, decisions.
3. **Reflect** – what worked? what didn’t? what surprised me?
4. **Redact** – I strip out anything sensitive, then save it.
5. **Publish** – the clean version goes into this repo.

This isn’t a complex workflow—it’s just **writing, reflecting, and sharing**.

## 📝 A Real (Redacted) Example

**What I actually wrote in my private notes:**  
> *"Spoke with Sarah and Mike about the payment gateway. Sarah is stressed about the API rate limits. We agreed to ask the cloud team for more quota."*

**What appears here:**  
> **Impediment Unblocking**  
> During a technical sync, a team member raised concerns about external API limits. I reached out to the infrastructure team to explore quota adjustments—turning a potential blocker into a simple request. The team felt relieved, and we moved forward without delay.
